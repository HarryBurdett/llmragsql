Each transaction is fuzzy-matched against your Opera customer and supplier accounts. The system handles name variations, abbreviations, and spelling differences automatically. It also learns from your corrections - improving accuracy with every import.

Matching & Learning Capabilities

  • Handles truncated names (bank limits to ~18 characters)
  • Recognizes "LTD", "LIMITED", "PLC" variations
  • Uses search keys and trading names for better matching
  • Pattern learning: remembers successful matches for future imports
  • Auto-detects refunds by checking for unallocated credit notes in Opera

Complete Double-Entry Posting

Every transaction creates full Opera postings: cashbook entries (aentry/atran), nominal ledger (ntran), ledger transfer files (anoml), and updates bank balances (nbank), customer/supplier balances, and nominal account balances (nacnt). Bank references from the statement are preserved for exact matching during reconciliation.

Flexible Partial Reconciliation

Not all statement lines need to match before you can start reconciling. The system supports partial reconciliation - post the entries you've matched now and complete the remaining items later in Opera Cashbook > Reconcile.

Partial Reconciliation Controls

  • Statement line numbers are assigned to matched entries (ae_tmpstat)
  • Opera's reconciled balance (nk_recbal) only advances on full reconciliation
  • Closing balance is stored in nk_reccfwd for carry-forward
  • Unmatched lines can be completed in Opera's native Cashbook > Reconcile screen
  • Next statement import is blocked until reconciliation is completed
