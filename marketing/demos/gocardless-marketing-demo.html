The Problem Every Finance Team Knows

Getting money in the door and keeping your books straight shouldn't consume half your week. But for most businesses using Opera, this is the reality.

❌

Chasing customers for payment wastes time. Reminder letters, follow-up emails, phone calls that go to voicemail. You know the invoice is due. They know the invoice is due. Yet here you are, chasing.

❌

Manual bank reconciliation of receipts. Money arrives in the bank. You open Opera. You try to match each receipt to each customer. Some descriptions are cryptic. Some are truncated. Some you just cannot identify.

❌

Posting individual receipts to the cashbook takes hours. Every payment means finding the customer, entering the amount, selecting the correct bank account, posting the receipt. Repeat 50 times.

❌

Allocating payments to invoices is tedious. Even after posting, you still need to allocate each receipt to the correct invoice. For customers with multiple outstanding invoices, this is painfully slow.

❌

Credit control calls that go unanswered. Your team spends hours on the phone chasing overdue balances. The customers who don't pay on time are the same ones who never pick up.

❌

Your credit control team could be doing something more valuable. Business owners and finance managers are tied up chasing money instead of focusing on growth, strategy, and customer relationships. Time spent on debt collection is time not spent on growing the business.

The Real Cost

A finance team processing 50 customer receipts per month can easily spend 8-10 hours on the cycle of chasing, receiving, posting, allocating, and reconciling. That is more than a full working day every month spent on tasks that should be automatic. Time your team could spend on higher-value work — growing the business, improving margins, building customer relationships.

How It Works

Four simple steps from outstanding invoice to cash in the bank and posted in Opera.

1

Customer Signs a Direct Debit Mandate

Send an email link. The customer authorises the mandate online in under 2 minutes. This is a one-time setup — once signed, you can collect against it indefinitely.

2

You Select Outstanding Invoices to Collect

Review what's due and select which invoices to collect. Multiple invoices per customer are combined into a single collection. One click to request payment.

3

GoCardless Collects the Money

GoCardless debits the customer's bank account via Direct Debit. The customer sees your company name on their bank statement. Funds are batched and paid out to your bank.

4

Everything Posts to Opera Automatically

Sales receipts, invoice allocations, fee postings, VAT tracking, cashbook entries, nominal ledger — all updated automatically when you import the payout. One click.

VAT You Might Be Missing

GoCardless fees are subject to VAT, and that VAT is reclaimable as input tax. Many businesses overlook this because the fee deduction is buried in the payout detail. At £20,000/month in collections, you could be losing around £100/month — over £1,200/year — in unclaimed VAT.

How? The Control Bank

All the detail — individual customer receipts, fee postings, VAT — is posted to a GoCardless clearing account in Opera. A single net transfer then moves the payout total to your real bank account. One Opera entry matches one bank statement line. The clearing account always nets to zero. All the detail is preserved for audit, but your bank reconciliation is clean.

Direct Debit means you collect on the date you choose — not when the customer remembers. Money comes in before it becomes overdue, not weeks or months after. Average debtor days drop significantly. The return on investment is not marginal — it is overwhelming. Most businesses recoup the Direct Debit fees many times over through time savings, improved collection rates, and eliminated bad debt alone.
